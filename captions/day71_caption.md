# Day 71 Caption — "Reflection meditation: questions as practice" (TUTORIAL)

## TikTok (@stillmeditation)

Ask yourself one question. Then sit with it for 10 minutes. Don't try to answer it. Just hold it.

This is reflection meditation — one of the oldest contemplative practices in the world, and one of the least talked-about. Most people think meditation means emptying the mind. Reflection meditation does the opposite. It uses a question as the anchor. The inquiry itself becomes the object of attention.

The key distinction is this: you are not trying to think your way to an answer. You are sitting with the question the way you would sit with a piece of music — not analyzing it, just letting it move through you. The analytical mind rests. Something deeper starts to respond.

→ Step 1: Choose one question. Keep it personal and open-ended. Classic starting points: "What do I really want?" or "What am I afraid of?" or "What am I avoiding right now?" One question only. More than one splits the attention and you end up in your head.

→ Step 2: Sit and wait. Close your eyes. Breathe normally. Hold the question in the center of your awareness like you are holding a lit candle — present but still. Do not chase an answer. Do not brainstorm. Just sit with the question and listen inward.

→ Step 3: Return. When the mind wanders into problem-solving mode, gently return to the question. Not the answer — the question. The return itself is the practice.

What tends to happen: in the first few minutes, the thinking mind rushes in with the first available answer. That answer is usually the surface one — the safe, familiar response. If you keep sitting, something quieter often surfaces underneath it. That second voice is the one worth listening to.

This is contemplative meditation. The question is not a problem to solve. It is a doorway. You are practicing the art of not rushing through it.

Tonight: one question. Ten minutes. Sit with it.
