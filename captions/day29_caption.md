# Day 29 Caption — "Loving-kindness: the meditation that changes how you feel about yourself" (TUTORIAL)

## TikTok (@stillmeditation)

Most people are kind to everyone except themselves.

Loving-kindness meditation is the only practice with hard evidence that it changes how you feel about yourself in weeks, not years.

It is not woo. It is a structured cognitive workout that softens the inner critic and rewires the brain's default response to your own mistakes.

Here is the full practice. Five minutes is enough.

→ Sit quietly. Close your eyes. Take three slow breaths and let your shoulders drop. You do not need to clear your mind. You just need to be still.

→ Place a hand on your chest if it helps you focus. Bring your attention to yourself — the version of you sitting here right now.

→ Repeat these four phrases silently, slowly, with space between each one:
"May I be happy."
"May I be healthy."
"May I be safe."
"May I live with ease."

→ Feel each phrase land. Do not rush through them like a checklist. Pause and let the meaning register in the body. If you feel resistance, awkwardness, or even guilt — that is the practice working. Keep going.

→ Repeat the cycle for three to five minutes. When your mind drifts, return to the phrases without judgment.

→ When you are ready, extend the same wishes outward. To someone you love. Then to someone neutral. Then to someone difficult. Then to all beings.

A 2008 study tracked people doing this for seven weeks. The result: measurable increases in positive emotions, social connection, and even purpose in life. Brain scans showed structural changes in regions linked to empathy and emotional regulation.

You are not lying to yourself when you say these phrases. You are practicing a different default. The brain learns what you repeat.

Be patient with the awkwardness. The shift is real.
