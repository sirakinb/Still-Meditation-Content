# Day 58 Caption — "Extended breathwork session: 10-minute flow" (TUTORIAL)

## TikTok (@stillmeditation)

Most people do a few deep breaths and call it breathwork. That is not a session. A session has a beginning, a middle, and an end — and each phase does something different to your nervous system.

Here is a complete 10-minute breathwork flow used by practitioners. Every minute has a purpose.

→ Min 1–2: Diaphragmatic warm-up. Place one hand on your belly. Breathe so only your belly moves — not your chest. Long slow inhales, slow exhales. This primes the diaphragm and signals the parasympathetic system to begin shifting. You are warming the engine before driving it.

→ Min 3–5: Alternate nostril breathing. Right hand up. Thumb to close the right nostril, inhale left. Ring finger to close the left nostril, exhale right. Inhale right. Close right, exhale left. That is one cycle. Repeat for two minutes. Alternate nostril breathing balances activity in both cerebral hemispheres and reduces anxiety measurably — a 2018 study in the Journal of Clinical Psychology found it lowered cortisol faster than deep breathing alone.

→ Min 6–8: Ujjayi with a 2:1 ratio. Ujjayi is an ocean-sounding breath made by gently constricting the back of the throat — like fogging a mirror but with your mouth closed. Inhale for 4 counts. Exhale for 8. The 2:1 exhale-to-inhale ratio activates the vagus nerve and drives a deeper parasympathetic response than even breathing. This is where the session earns its depth.

→ Min 9–10: Return to natural breath and observe. Stop directing the breath entirely. Let it find its own rhythm. Sit and watch it without interfering. Notice how the body breathes on its own after being trained for eight minutes. That stillness in the last two minutes is the whole point — you worked to arrive here.

This is how breathwork practitioners structure a real session. Not one technique done randomly, but four phases sequenced to take the nervous system from ordinary to deeply regulated.

Ten minutes. Tonight.
