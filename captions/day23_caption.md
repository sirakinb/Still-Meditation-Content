# Day 23 Caption — "Walking Meditation: Mindfulness Without Sitting" (TUTORIAL)

## TikTok (@stillmeditation)

Hate sitting still? This meditation is for you.

Walking meditation isn't a backup plan for people who can't sit. It's a legitimate practice — and it builds a different kind of attention than seated meditation does.

Here's how to do it:

→ Find a path 10 to 20 steps long. Indoors or outside. Doesn't matter.

→ Walk slower than normal. Deliberate. Every step is intentional.

→ Feel three phases per step: lifting, moving, placing. That's it. That's the whole technique.

→ Sync your breath. Inhale as you lift. Exhale as you place. One breath per step.

→ When your mind wanders — and it will — the breath and the physical sensation pull it back. You have two anchors instead of one.

Why it works:

Seated meditation trains you to direct attention inward. Walking meditation trains outward, embodied awareness — sensing your body moving through space, feeling the ground, noticing weight shift. Both practices create measurable changes in the prefrontal cortex. They're not competing. They're complementary.

If you've been struggling to sit still for 10 minutes, walking meditation is not the easier option. It's the different option. Some people find it harder to stay present while walking than while sitting. That difficulty is the practice.

Start with 10 minutes. Slow steps. No destination.
