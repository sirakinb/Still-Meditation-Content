# Day 26 Caption — "The mindful meal challenge" (CHALLENGE)

## TikTok (@stillmeditation)

Eat one meal today without your phone.

That's the whole challenge.

Most people scroll through every meal. You finish the plate without ever tasting it. Your stomach is full but your brain didn't register the food, so you stay hungry an hour later.

This is one of the simplest forms of meditation that exists. No cushion. No timer. No app. Just you and what's in front of you.

Here's the playbook:

→ Before the first bite: take three slow breaths. Look at your food. Notice the colors and the steam.

→ First bite: notice texture, temperature, and flavor before you chew. Then chew slowly. Most people chew 4-7 times. Try 15-20.

→ Between every bite: put the fork down. Hands on the table. Breathe.

→ When your mind pulls toward your phone or the next thing on your list, return your attention to the food. That noticing is the meditation.

Why this works:

Mindful eating activates the same parasympathetic shift as a sitting meditation, but with a built-in anchor — taste. You can't fake it. The flavor is either there or it isn't. Your nervous system has no choice but to land in the present.

People also report eating less, finishing more satisfied, and noticing fullness signals they've been overriding for years.

Pick one meal today. Lunch is easiest. Phone face-down across the room.

That's it. That's the practice.
