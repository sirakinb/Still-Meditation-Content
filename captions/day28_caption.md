# Day 28 Caption — "The secret to a daily practice isn't discipline — it's design" (QUICK TIP)

## TikTok (@stillmeditation)

The people who meditate every day are not more disciplined than you.

They have just designed their day so meditation is the path of least resistance.

Discipline is a willpower tax. It runs out by 3 PM.

Design is a system. It runs whether you feel like it or not.

Here is how to design a daily practice that actually sticks:

→ Anchor it. Same time. Same place. Attach it to something you already do — right after your morning coffee, right before your shower, right when you sit at your desk. Your brain stops asking "should I meditate?" and starts treating it as part of the existing chain.

→ Start absurdly small. Not 20 minutes. Not 10. Two. The goal for the first 30 days is not depth — it is showing up. A 2-minute session you actually do beats a 20-minute session you skip.

→ Never miss twice in a row. One miss is life. Two misses is a new habit forming. Researchers who tracked habit formation found that a single skipped day did not break the streak — but two consecutive skips reset it almost entirely. Protect the second day at all costs.

→ Track it. A simple X on a calendar works. Visible streaks trigger a small dopamine hit and create loss aversion — you do not want to break the chain.

→ Remove the friction. Keep the cushion in plain sight. Have the app on your home screen. The decision to start should take zero seconds. Every barrier between you and the cushion is a chance to bail.

The technique you use barely matters in the first month. The system that gets you to the cushion is everything.

Stop relying on motivation. Build the design.
