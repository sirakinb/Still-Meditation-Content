# Day 45 Caption — "Halfway there: your 45-day meditation assessment" (CHECK-IN)

## TikTok (@stillmeditation)

You are halfway through a 90-day transformation. Most people never check if the work is working. That is why most people never know if they are improving. Today we measure.

This is not a vibe check. This is a four-question scan of the things meditation is supposed to actually change. Be honest. The score only matters if it is real.

Rate yourself one to ten on each.

→ Question one. Can you sit for ten minutes without checking the time? At day one most people last two minutes before their hand reaches for their phone. By day 45 that fidget reflex should be noticeably weaker. If you can sit through a full session without your mind racing toward the clock, your attention span is rebuilding.

→ Question two. Do you notice thoughts before reacting? This is the core skill. The gap between stimulus and response. Someone cuts you off in traffic, your boss sends a snippy email, your partner says the wrong thing. Do you catch the reaction forming before it leaves your mouth? If yes, your prefrontal cortex is getting louder than your amygdala.

→ Question three. Has your sleep improved? Falling asleep faster, fewer 3 a.m. wakeups, waking up less wired. Meditation lowers baseline cortisol and quiets the default mode network. Better sleep is one of the earliest measurable wins, usually showing up between week three and week six.

→ Question four. Do you catch tension in your body during the day? Tight jaw, shoulders by your ears, shallow breathing. Most people only notice tension after a headache. A trained practitioner catches it in real time and releases it. This is interoception, and it grows with every body scan you do.

Add up your scores. If several of these are above five, you are right on track. If most are below five, the practice is happening but the dosage may be too low. Bump to ten minutes daily for the next two weeks.

You are not behind. You are 45 days deeper than you were.

Forty-five to go.
