# Day 39 Caption — "4 breathing patterns for 4 different situations" (QUICK TIP)

## TikTok (@stillmeditation)

Your breath is the only nervous system remote control you carry everywhere. Most people use one button.

The trick is not "breathe more." It is matching the right pattern to the state you actually want. Save this — you will use it today.

→ Need calm? Use 4-7-8. Inhale through the nose for 4. Hold for 7. Exhale through the mouth for 8. Four rounds. The long exhale floods your system with the "you are safe" signal. Best used before bed or when anxiety is climbing.

→ Need focus? Use box breathing. Inhale 4, hold 4, exhale 4, hold 4. Repeat for 2 minutes. Used by Navy SEALs before missions. Resets your attention without making you drowsy. Best before a hard task or important meeting.

→ Need energy? Use quick inhales, slow exhales. Three short sharp inhales through the nose, then one long exhale through the mouth. Repeat 10 times. Wakes you up without caffeine. Best at 3pm when the crash hits or before a workout.

→ Need balance? Use coherent breathing. Inhale 5 seconds, exhale 5 seconds. That is six breaths per minute. Hold for 5 minutes. This is the rhythm that maximizes heart rate variability — the single best marker of stress resilience. Best as a daily reset.

The four buttons cover almost every state you will be in today. Calm, focus, energy, balance. Pick the one that matches what you need right now.

Your breath is a tool. Use the right one.
