# Day 21 Caption — "21 Days of Meditation: What's Changed" (CHECK-IN)

## TikTok (@stillmeditation)

3 weeks in. Let's talk about what's actually happening.

21 days ago you didn't know if this would work. You still might feel like a beginner. You still might skip days. You still might think your mind is "too busy" to meditate.

But something has shifted. Most people miss it because the changes are quiet.

Here's what 3 weeks of meditation is actually doing to you:

→ You catch yourself mid-thought-spiral. You start spinning about something, then suddenly notice you're spinning. That noticing used to take hours. Now it takes seconds. That's the gap between you and your thoughts widening.

→ You're slightly less reactive to small annoyances. The slow driver. The loud coworker. The spilled coffee. They still happen — but the 0-to-100 reaction is shorter. You pause before you snap. Sometimes you don't snap at all.

→ Sleep onset is easier. You're falling asleep faster. Not every night, but enough nights to notice. Your nervous system is learning how to power down on command.

→ You're finding tension you never knew you carried. A clenched jaw at 2pm. Shoulders glued to your ears during emails. A held breath when you read bad news. You're not more tense — you're more aware. Awareness is step one of release.

The habit is forming. Research suggests 66 days for full automaticity — you're 1/3 of the way there. Don't stop now.
