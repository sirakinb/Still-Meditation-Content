# Day 85 Caption — "Build your personal meditation practice plan" (TUTORIAL)

## TikTok (@stillmeditation)

You've learned 15 techniques over the last 84 days. Most people stop there. The ones who transform are the ones who build a practice.

A personal meditation practice is not a fixed routine you found online. It is a structure you design around how your mind actually works. And the structure that lasts is not complicated — it has three layers.

→ Layer 1: Ground it. Every session starts with a grounding technique. Breath anchor or body scan. This is the entry point. It brings your scattered attention into the body. Without it, you are trying to meditate on top of noise. Give this 3 to 5 minutes every time.

→ Layer 2: Open the heart. After you are settled, add a heart-based practice. Loving-kindness or compassion meditation. This is the layer most people skip, and it is the layer most responsible for the mood and emotional resilience benefits research keeps measuring. Two minutes is enough if you mean it.

→ Layer 3: Expand awareness. The third layer is where the depth lives. Open awareness or choiceless awareness — no object, no target, no technique. Just presence watching itself. This is where the nervous system learns to stop gripping. Even three minutes here changes the texture of your day.

Your daily practice does not need to be long. Ten minutes covers all three layers if you move with intention. Fifteen gives you room to breathe inside each one.

One more variable: rotate your techniques weekly. If you ground with breath anchor this week, use body scan next week. Rotation keeps the practice fresh and trains the mind in multiple modes instead of locking into one comfort zone.

Pick a time. Same time every day. The brain learns through repetition of context, not just repetition of technique. Same chair, same time, same signal — and the nervous system starts quieting before you even close your eyes.

You have the tools. Now build the ritual.
