# Day 78 Caption — "The dose-response curve: how much meditation is enough" (SCIENCE)

## TikTok (@stillmeditation)

After 90 days, here is the minimum you need to keep every gain you have made. It is less than you think.

The dose-response curve is one of the most important findings in meditation research. More practice produces more benefit — but only up to a point. Beyond that point, the curve flattens. You are spending more time for the same return.

→ What the research shows. Studies across mindfulness, MBSR, and transcendental meditation consistently find that 10 to 20 minutes of daily practice is sufficient to maintain the full range of benefits — reduced cortisol, improved focus, better emotional regulation, lower anxiety, and structural brain changes. You do not need an hour to keep what you have built.

→ Where the curve flattens. Around 45 minutes per session, benefits plateau for most practitioners. This does not mean longer sits are worthless — dedicated retreats and extended practice have their own value — but for daily maintenance, duration past that point yields diminishing returns.

→ Why consistency beats duration. A landmark review in Psychological Science found that daily short sessions outperform infrequent long sessions across virtually every measured outcome. The brain consolidates the pattern through repetition, not volume. Ten minutes every day for a year is worth more than two-hour sessions twice a week.

→ Your minimum dose. Science says 10 to 15 minutes daily is enough to maintain everything you have built over the past 78 days. That is what keeps gray matter density stable. That is what keeps your stress response calibrated. That is what keeps your attention trainable.

You have already proven you can do this. The question after Day 90 is not whether to continue — it is just how to fit your minimum dose into whatever comes next.

Ten minutes a day is a lifetime practice.
