# Day 73 Caption — "The Wim Hof method: breathwork's final boss" (TUTORIAL)

## TikTok (@stillmeditation)

This breathing technique is so intense there are medical safety rules. Most people have never tried it. Once you do, you understand why Wim Hof climbed Everest in shorts.

The Wim Hof method is a controlled hyperventilation protocol developed by Dutch athlete Wim Hof. Research from Radboud University (2014) showed that practitioners could voluntarily suppress their immune response — something previously thought impossible. The mechanism is elevated oxygen saturation combined with mild alkalosis, which activates the sympathetic nervous system and floods the bloodstream with adrenaline.

Here is the full protocol. Three rounds. Fifteen minutes. Always seated or lying down.

→ Step 1: 30 deep breaths. Full inhale through nose or mouth, full chest expansion. Relaxed exhale — just let it fall. No force on the out-breath. You may feel tingling, lightheadedness, or warmth. That is normal. Keep going.

→ Step 2: After breath 30, exhale fully and hold on empty lungs. Do not force the hold — just wait. When the urge to breathe becomes real, move to Step 3.

→ Step 3: Take one deep recovery inhale. Hold it for 15 seconds. Then release fully. That is one complete round.

Repeat 3 rounds total.

By round three, most practitioners report a deep buzzing calm — almost like a natural high. Heart rate slows, body temperature drops slightly, and the mind becomes unusually still.

Critical safety rules that cannot be skipped. NEVER practice near water — passing out is possible. NEVER practice standing. Always on an empty stomach. Sit on the floor or lie flat. This is an advanced technique for experienced practitioners who already have a consistent meditation practice.

If you are new to breathwork, start with box breathing or the physiological sigh before attempting this.

Three rounds. The same fifteen minutes you spend scrolling.
