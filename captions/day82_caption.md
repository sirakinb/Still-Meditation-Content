# Day 82 Caption — "The 3-breath reset: your anytime anywhere practice" (QUICK TIP)

## TikTok (@stillmeditation)

The most useful meditation technique fits in 10 seconds.

Most people think meditation requires a quiet room, a timer, and 20 minutes they don't have. But the 3-breath reset lives inside the gaps of your day. Between meetings. At a red light. Before you open a difficult email. Three breaths. That's the whole practice.

Each breath has one job.

→ Breath 1 — Arrive: Inhale slowly and notice where you are. Feel your feet on the floor. Hear the sounds around you. You're not thinking about what just happened or what comes next. You're just here.

→ Breath 2 — Allow: Exhale completely. Whatever you're feeling — stressed, distracted, anxious, tired — you're not fighting it. You're acknowledging it. "There is tension here." That single move shifts you from reactive to aware.

→ Breath 3 — Align: One more breath. As you inhale, set a quiet intention for the next moment. I want to listen. I choose calm. I'm going to be fully present. That's it. You don't need a mantra. One clear intention is enough.

The research backs this up. A 2017 study from Stanford found that brief intentional breathing pauses reduce amygdala reactivity — the brain region that triggers stress responses — within seconds. You don't need five minutes of coherent breathing to shift your state. Three breaths with intention is enough to interrupt the stress cycle and reorient the nervous system.

Use it every time you transition from one thing to another. The 3-breath reset turns every transition into a micro-reset — and you have dozens of those every day.

Ten seconds. Anywhere. Every time.
