# Day 35 Caption — "5 weeks in and you probably haven't noticed the biggest changes" (CHECK-IN)

## TikTok (@stillmeditation)

5 weeks in. You probably feel like nothing has changed.

That is normal. The biggest shifts from a meditation practice are quiet. They show up first in how other people experience you, not in how you feel sitting on the cushion.

Here is what to look for at the 5-week mark.

→ You are less reactive to criticism. Someone says something sharp and you do not snap back. You hear it. You think. You respond. The old version of you would have been defensive in milliseconds.

→ You are more patient in conversations. You stop interrupting. You let people finish. You actually listen instead of waiting for your turn to talk.

→ You pause before responding. A half-second gap shows up between stimulus and reaction. That gap is everything. That is where freedom lives.

→ Background anxiety drops to a hum. It is not gone. It is just quieter. You can feel it without being controlled by it. Lower-grade. Manageable.

→ Practice starts feeling different. It used to be a should. Now it is a want. You miss it on days you skip it. That is the turn.

The reason you have not noticed is that you live inside your own nervous system. The shifts are gradual. You normalize them fast. Ask the people closest to you. They saw it weeks ago.

Keep going. The next 5 weeks compound on this.
