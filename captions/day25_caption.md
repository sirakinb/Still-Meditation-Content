# Day 25 Caption — "Cyclic sighing: the Stanford-approved calm hack" (TUTORIAL)

## TikTok (@stillmeditation)

Stanford found the #1 breathing technique for stress.

It's not box breathing. It's not Wim Hof. It's not 4-7-8.

It's called cyclic sighing.

In 2023, Stanford and Andrew Huberman ran a controlled study comparing four techniques: cyclic sighing, box breathing, cyclic hyperventilation, and mindfulness meditation. Each group did just 5 minutes a day for a month.

Cyclic sighing won every measurable category — biggest mood boost, lowest anxiety, lowest resting heart rate.

Here's the technique:

→ Inhale through your nose. One full breath.

→ Without exhaling, take a small second sip of air through your nose on top of it. Your lungs are now maximally full.

→ Exhale slowly through your mouth. Make the exhale longer than both inhales combined. Empty all the way out.

→ Repeat for 5 minutes.

Why this beats other techniques:

The double inhale reopens tiny collapsed air sacs (alveoli) deep in your lungs that build up when you're stressed and shallow-breathing. The extended exhale dumps CO2 fast, which is the actual signal your nervous system uses to downshift.

Most people feel a measurable shift within 60 seconds. Within 5 minutes, the parasympathetic system has fully taken over.

The reason this is the best stress technique on paper isn't because it's exotic. It's because the mechanism is mechanical, not mental. You're physically resetting your lungs and CO2 levels. There's nothing to "do right."

Try it the next time you feel the spike coming.
