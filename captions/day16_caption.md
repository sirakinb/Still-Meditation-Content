# Day 16 Caption — "Why Meditation Feels Harder Before It Feels Better" (SCIENCE)

## TikTok (@stillmeditation)

Week 2-3 is when most people quit meditation. Here's why they shouldn't.

You're not getting worse. You're waking up.

Your mind isn't suddenly louder than it was before — you're finally hearing it. The thoughts were always there. The tension was always there. The mental noise was always there. Meditation didn't create any of it.

Think of it like this:

→ Imagine a messy room in the dark. You can't see the clutter, so you assume everything is fine.
→ Now turn on the light. Suddenly you see everything. Every pile. Every mess. It feels overwhelming — but nothing actually changed.
→ The mess was always there. The light just made it visible.

That's what meditation does. It doesn't make your mind busier. It makes you aware of how busy your mind has always been. And that awareness feels like a setback — but it's actually the breakthrough.

You cannot change what you cannot see. Every moment of noticing — even noticing how hard this is — is the practice working. The discomfort is the proof.

Week 4 is when things start to shift. The noticing gets quieter. The reactivity slows down. You catch yourself before the spiral. But only if you don't quit at Week 3.

Save this for when you want to quit. Follow @stillmeditation for Day 17.
