# Day 87 Caption — "Build your personal breathwork routine" (TUTORIAL)

## TikTok (@stillmeditation)

Most people have one breathing technique they use sometimes. What you actually need is a breathwork system designed around your day.

The research on breathwork is specific: different techniques activate different branches of your nervous system at different speeds. Trying to use one technique for everything is like using the same setting on a thermostat for summer and winter. The technique has to match the moment.

Here is a four-window framework that covers your entire day without adding more than fifteen minutes total.

→ Morning energy. Two minutes of diaphragmatic breathing to fully activate the respiratory system and drive CO2 out. Then three minutes of box breathing — four counts in, four hold, four out, four hold. This sequence wakes the prefrontal cortex before your phone does. Focus lands faster. Decision quality in the first hour of the day improves.

→ Afternoon reset. Two minutes of coherent breathing: five seconds in, five seconds out through the nose. This specific ratio — discovered by researchers at the Heart Math Institute — maximises heart rate variability and balances the sympathetic and parasympathetic systems simultaneously. Use it before a difficult meeting, at a stress spike, or simply at 2pm when the afternoon fog rolls in.

→ Evening wind-down. Four-seven-eight breathing — four counts in, hold for seven, exhale for eight. The long exhale activates the vagus nerve and pulls cortisol down. Follow it with a five-minute body scan. By the time you reach your feet, your brain will have shifted into the frequency range where deep sleep becomes easy.

→ Weekend deep practice. Fifteen minutes using all three techniques in sequence with no agenda. This is where integration happens. Short daily windows build skill. The longer weekend session builds depth.

The key variable is adaptation. If mornings are already calm, use your morning window for heart-rate coherence instead. If evenings are your highest stress point, extend the 4-7-8 section. This framework is a starting point, not a prescription.

Design it once. Practice it daily. Your nervous system will start expecting the cues — and responding faster every week.
