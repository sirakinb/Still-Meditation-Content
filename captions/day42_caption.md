# Day 42 Caption — "The 7-day loving-kindness challenge" (CHALLENGE)

## TikTok (@stillmeditation)

Send kindness to a different person each day this week. Watch what happens to your nervous system by Day 7.

Loving-kindness meditation is one of the most studied practices in neuroscience. Just two weeks of it increases vagal tone, reduces inflammation markers, and rewires the brain to feel more connected to other people. The trick is that you cannot stay in your comfort zone. The growth happens when you start sending kindness to people who are not easy to love.

Here is the 7-day protocol. Two minutes a day. Repeat one phrase silently for each person.

The phrase: "May you be happy. May you be safe. May you be free from suffering."

→ Day 1: Yourself. This is where everyone gets stuck. Most people skip themselves. Do not skip yourself. You cannot pour from a dry well. Put your hand on your chest and say the phrase to you.

→ Day 2: Someone you deeply love. Easy mode. Picture them clearly. A parent, a partner, a best friend, a pet. Feel how natural the warmth comes. That same warmth is what you are training.

→ Day 3: A close friend. Notice the difference between Day 2 and Day 3 — the love is just as real, slightly less automatic.

→ Day 4: A coworker you barely know. The cashier you saw yesterday. The neighbor whose name you forgot. The brain learns to extend warmth to people it has no emotional reason to care about.

→ Day 5: A complete stranger. The person ahead of you in line. The driver in the next car. Someone you have never spoken to. This is where the practice starts to feel different.

→ Day 6: Someone you find difficult. The hard one. An ex, a family member you clash with, a boss who frustrates you. You are not excusing them. You are training your nervous system to not be hijacked by them.

→ Day 7: All beings everywhere. Open the circle wide. Every person on the planet, in this exact moment, struggling with something. Send the phrase out to all of them.

By Day 7, something quietly shifts. The world stops feeling like it's against you. That shift is the practice doing its work.

One person a day. Two minutes. Start tonight.
