# Day 84 Caption — "12 weeks: the comprehensive assessment" (CHECK-IN)

## TikTok (@stillmeditation)

12 weeks of practice. Here's your meditation report card.

Most people never stop to measure. They either feel like it's working or they doubt it. Today you do neither. Today you assess.

There are four domains worth checking. Rate yourself honestly in each one.

PHYSICAL. Sleep quality — is it deeper, longer, easier to fall into? Muscle tension — do you carry less of it by default? Resting heart rate — has it dropped even a few beats? Harvard research shows 8 weeks of daily practice measurably reduces cortisol and lowers resting heart rate. At 12 weeks, those changes compound.

MENTAL. Focus duration — can you hold attention longer before it scatters? Reactivity speed — do you pause before snapping, before spiraling? Thought awareness — do you notice when you've been hijacked by a thought loop? A 2019 Mindfulness study found 3 months of practice increased sustained attention scores by 16 percent.

EMOTIONAL. Compassion capacity — can you hold warmth for people you find difficult? Emotional resilience — do you recover from setbacks faster than you used to? Equanimity — is there a steadier baseline underneath the day's waves? These are not soft metrics. They show up in your relationships, your work, and your body.

SPIRITUAL. Sense of spaciousness — does life feel less compressed, less urgent? Connection — do you feel part of something larger than your own problems? Presence — are you arriving in more moments instead of already somewhere else? These are the quietest changes. They are also the deepest.

Rate yourself in each area from one to ten. Notice where the biggest gaps were when you started. Notice where you've grown the most. That asymmetry tells you something true about your practice and about you.

The gains you've made are yours. No one can take them back.
