# Day 66 Caption — "Meditation on emotions: the R.A.I.N. technique" (TUTORIAL)

## TikTok (@stillmeditation)

You can't think your way out of a strong emotion. But you can rain on it.

R.A.I.N. is a four-step mindfulness technique developed by meditation teacher Tara Brach. It doesn't suppress what you're feeling. It doesn't tell you to calm down. It teaches you to meet the emotion directly — and in doing so, take away its power to run the show.

Here is how it works.

→ R: Recognize. Name what's happening without editorializing. Not "I'm falling apart" — just "I notice fear" or "I notice anger." Labeling an emotion activates the prefrontal cortex and reduces amygdala reactivity. That naming alone interrupts the automatic loop.

→ A: Allow. Let the emotion be there. This is the hardest step. Every instinct says to push it away, fix it, or distract yourself. But resistance amplifies. When you stop fighting the feeling and simply let it exist, something shifts. It doesn't get bigger. Often, it starts to soften.

→ I: Investigate with kindness. Where do you feel this in your body? Is there tightness in your chest? Heat in your face? Tension in your jaw? This step moves attention from the story your mind is telling to the raw physical sensation. You're not analyzing — you're exploring, with the same care you'd give a friend who's hurting.

→ N: Non-Identification. The emotion is a weather pattern passing through. It is not your identity. "I am angry" fuses you with the feeling. "I notice anger arising" creates just enough distance to choose your response. That space is where freedom lives.

You don't need to be in crisis to practice this. The next time any strong emotion arises — frustration, worry, loneliness — pause and move through each step. It takes less than two minutes. The results compound over time.

Feelings don't disappear when ignored. They get louder. But when you shine a light on them, they lose their grip.
