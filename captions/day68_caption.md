# Day 68 Caption — "Choiceless awareness: the most advanced basic skill" (TUTORIAL)

## TikTok (@stillmeditation)

Most meditation teaches you to choose. Focus on the breath. Return to the breath. Hold attention steady.

Choiceless awareness does the opposite. You stop choosing what to pay attention to — and let awareness move on its own.

It sounds simple. It is the most advanced basic skill in meditation. Here is how to practice it.

→ Begin with the breath. Sit for three minutes and follow each inhale and exhale. This settles the mind and builds a stable base.

→ Then release all choosing. Stop steering attention toward anything. Stop returning to the breath. Let go of the wheel entirely.

→ Whatever becomes most prominent, let awareness go there naturally. A sound in the room. A passing thought. A sensation in the body. You do not pick it. It pulls you.

→ When it fades, let the next prominent experience take its place. You are not chasing anything. You are simply present to what is loudest in this moment, then the next.

Think of it like a spotlight that moves on its own. You are not aiming the light. You are watching where it lands.

This trains a profound shift: from controlling your experience to allowing it. Most of our suffering comes from the constant effort to manage what we notice. Choiceless awareness shows you that attention can take care of itself.

Start with one minute after your breath practice. Build from there.
