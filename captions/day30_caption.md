# Day 30 Caption — "Loving-kindness meditation lights up your brain like nothing else" (SCIENCE)

## TikTok (@stillmeditation)

Most meditations train attention.

Loving-kindness meditation trains the social brain — and the fMRI evidence is some of the strongest in the entire field.

When researchers scan people doing this practice, three regions change in ways no other meditation produces.

→ The insula. This is your empathy hub. It tracks what other people feel and reflects it inside your own body. In loving-kindness practitioners it fires faster and stronger when they encounter another person's emotion. You actually feel them more clearly.

→ The temporal parietal junction. The TPJ is the brain region that lets you take someone else's perspective. It is what stops you from assuming your view is the only view. Regular practice thickens it. You see past your own head.

→ The amygdala. The threat detector. Loving-kindness reduces its baseline reactivity. You stop bracing for every interaction like it might hurt you. The world feels less like a threat.

The behavioral results match the brain scans. Practitioners show measurably more compassion, more cooperative behavior in lab tasks, and lower implicit bias on tests they cannot game.

This is not a vague mood boost. It is structural change in the parts of the brain that decide how you treat people — including yourself.

The dose is small. The shift is real.
