# Day 62 Caption — "Meditation and the default mode network" (SCIENCE)

## TikTok (@stillmeditation)

Scientists found the brain network responsible for overthinking. Meditation quiets it.

It is called the Default Mode Network — the DMN. It is the set of brain regions that lights up when you are not focused on anything in particular. No task, no goal, just your mind left to its own devices. And here is the catch: your mind left to its own devices rarely sits in peace. It wanders. It replays old conversations. It drafts arguments you will never have. It worries about a version of you that does not exist yet.

That is the DMN at work. It is the neural home of self-referential thinking — the endless internal monologue about me, my past, my future, my problems. A little of it is healthy. Too much of it is rumination, anxiety, and the restless monkey mind you have been fighting your whole life.

Then came the research.

→ In 2011, neuroscientists at Yale led by Judson Brewer scanned the brains of experienced meditators and non-meditators. The meditators showed measurably less activity in the main hubs of the Default Mode Network — during meditation AND at rest.

→ It went deeper than quieter. When the DMN did activate, the meditators' brains recovered faster. Their attention and self-control circuits stayed connected to the DMN, catching the mind-wandering and reeling it back in before it spiraled.

→ This is the neural signature of "letting go." Not forcing the mind blank. Not white-knuckling your thoughts into silence. Just a brain that has practiced noticing when it has drifted, and gently returning — over and over — until that return becomes automatic.

You are not stuck with the brain you have. The DMN is trainable. Every time you sit, notice you have wandered, and come back to the breath, you are rewiring the exact network responsible for your overthinking.

The monkey mind is not a character flaw. It is a network. And networks can be trained.

Quiet the noise. One sit at a time.
