# Day 56 Caption — "Mantra meditation: the power of repetition" (TUTORIAL)

## TikTok (@stillmeditation)

One word. Repeated silently. For ten minutes. That is the entire instruction.

It sounds too simple to work. It works because of how simple it is.

Your thinking mind is always in motion. It needs something to do. When you sit down to meditate and tell it to just be quiet, it rebels — spinning up plans, replaying conversations, cataloguing anxieties. The harder you push, the louder it gets.

Mantra meditation sidesteps that fight entirely. You give the mind a job. A gentle, repetitive job. And while it is busy doing that job, something underneath goes quiet.

→ Choose your word. It can be anything that resonates: "peace," "om," "let go," "still," "here," "release." There is no correct word. There is only the word that feels right to you right now.

→ Sit and close your eyes. Breathe naturally without controlling it. You are not doing a breathing exercise. You are just breathing.

→ With each exhale, repeat your word silently in your mind. Not out loud. Not even in a whisper. Just the internal echo of the word, timed loosely to the outbreath.

→ When a thought arises — and thoughts will arise, constantly — notice it without judgment. Then return to your word. That return is not a failure. That return is the practice. Every time you come back, you are strengthening exactly the mental muscle meditation is designed to build.

→ Do this for ten minutes. No music, no guidance after the first few breaths. Just you and your word.

In the gaps between repetitions, something opens. A brief pocket of quiet. It might last half a second the first time. With practice it stretches. That pocket is what you are looking for — not through effort, but through repetition wearing a path to stillness.

Mantra meditation is one of the oldest documented meditation techniques in the world. The reason it has survived five thousand years is simple: it works for almost everyone, because it meets the mind where it actually is rather than demanding it be somewhere it is not.

Try it tonight.
