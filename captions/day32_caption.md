# Day 32 Caption — "Send kindness to someone who annoys you" (CHALLENGE)

## TikTok (@stillmeditation)

Think of someone who irritates you.

Not the worst person in your life. Just the one who lives rent-free in your head right now. The coworker. The relative. The driver from yesterday.

Now try this.

There is a 2,500-year-old technique called metta — loving-kindness meditation. The challenge today is the harder version of it. You send well-wishes to a difficult person on purpose.

Here is the thing most people miss. This practice is not about them. It is about freeing YOUR mind from the resentment that is quietly costing you.

Brain scans show loving-kindness meditation reduces activity in the amygdala — the threat center. It increases gray matter in regions tied to empathy and emotional regulation. After eight weeks, practitioners report less anger, less rumination, and more relationships they actually enjoy.

Here is how to do it.

→ Step 1. Start with yourself. Sit. Close your eyes. Repeat silently: "May I be happy. May I be healthy. May I be at peace."

→ Step 2. Picture someone neutral. The barista. A neighbor. Send them the same phrases. "May they be happy. May they be healthy. May they be at peace."

→ Step 3. Now picture the person who annoys you. Same phrases. No softer words. No conditions.

It will feel strange. Even fake at first. Do it anyway. The neural circuit you are building is real, regardless of your initial sincerity.

Five minutes. One difficult person. Once today.

Notice what loosens.
