# Day 43 Caption — "Your heart rate variability is your stress scorecard" (SCIENCE)

## TikTok (@stillmeditation)

There is a single number that predicts how well you handle stress. It is not your heart rate. It is the tiny gaps between your heartbeats.

It is called heart rate variability — HRV — and it is the most honest readout you have of your nervous system. Higher HRV means your body can shift between calm and alert without getting stuck. Lower HRV means your stress system is gripping the wheel and forgetting how to let go.

→ What it actually measures. Your heart does not tick like a metronome. Between each beat there is a slightly different gap — sometimes 870 milliseconds, sometimes 920, sometimes 815. That variability is your vagus nerve doing its job. The more flexible the gaps, the more flexible your stress response.

→ Why it matters. Low HRV is a leading indicator of burnout, anxiety, poor sleep, and even cardiovascular risk. High HRV is linked to better focus, faster recovery, deeper sleep, and stronger emotional regulation. It is not a vanity number. It is a scorecard for how well your body is built to handle pressure.

→ The 2022 study. A randomized controlled trial published in Scientific Reports tracked adults doing 20 minutes a day of coherent breathing for 4 weeks. The result — significantly higher HRV, better cognitive performance, and lower self-reported stress. Same body. Different software.

→ The fix. Coherent breathing. Inhale for 5 seconds. Exhale for 5 seconds. Six breaths per minute. That cadence is the resonance frequency of your cardiovascular system — the rate at which your heart, lungs, and vagus nerve sync up and start cooperating instead of competing.

→ The protocol. 20 minutes a day. Four weeks. Check your HRV once a week — most smartwatches and Oura rings track it overnight. Watch the number climb as your nervous system relearns how to flex.

Your breath is the dial. HRV is the readout. You have always had access. Most people never check the gauge.
