# Day 67 Caption — "How breathwork boosts your immune system" (SCIENCE)

## TikTok (@stillmeditation)

Your breath can fight inflammation. Scientists proved it in 2014 — and the results were wild.

Researchers at Radboud University in the Netherlands trained a group of participants in breathwork for just 10 days. Then they did something extreme: they injected all participants — trained and untrained — with bacterial endotoxin. This is a substance that triggers the immune system to mount an inflammatory response. It causes flu-like symptoms: fever, chills, headaches, nausea.

Here is what happened next.

The breathwork-trained group produced 200% more anti-inflammatory cytokines than the control group. Their flu symptoms were dramatically reduced. Their bodies mounted a faster, more controlled immune response and recovered more quickly.

This was extraordinary. Until this study, scientists believed the autonomic nervous system — which governs heart rate, digestion, and immune function — was involuntary. Not something you could consciously influence.

The breathwork practitioners proved otherwise.

When you breathe deliberately — slow, deep, rhythmic breaths — you activate the parasympathetic nervous system. This reduces cortisol, lowers systemic inflammation, and signals the immune system to operate from a place of regulation rather than alarm. Ten days of practice was enough to transfer this ability durably.

You are not helpless against inflammation. You have a tool built into your body that you use 20,000 times a day.

You just have to use it differently.
