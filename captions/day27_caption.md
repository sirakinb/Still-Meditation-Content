# Day 27 Caption — "The best meditators in the world get distracted constantly" (MYTH)

## TikTok (@stillmeditation)

The best meditators in the world get distracted constantly.

Yes, even the monks with 50,000+ hours of practice.

Researchers have put long-term meditators inside fMRI machines and watched their default mode network — the "wandering mind" circuit — light up over and over while they meditate.

Their minds are not silent. Their minds wander.

So why are they considered masters?

Because they catch it faster.

That is the entire skill.

Here is what is actually happening when you meditate:

→ Your mind drifts. Always. That is what minds do.

→ At some point you notice you drifted. That moment of noticing is the rep.

→ You return your attention to the breath. Without judgment. Without "I'm so bad at this."

→ The mind drifts again. Faster than you expected.

→ You notice again. Slightly sooner this time.

That gap between drifting and noticing is the only thing that changes with practice. Not the drifting itself. The recognition.

Beginners might wander for 30 seconds before they catch it. Experienced practitioners catch it in 3. Monks catch it in less than 1.

Every catch is a brain rep. The prefrontal cortex strengthens. Emotional regulation improves. Attention sharpens.

If you sat down for 10 minutes and noticed your mind wandered 40 times, you did not have a bad session. You did 40 reps.

Stop chasing a silent mind. Start counting catches.
