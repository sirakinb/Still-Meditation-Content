# Day 41 Caption — "How to meditate when your emotions are intense" (TUTORIAL)

## TikTok (@stillmeditation)

Don't meditate your feelings away. Meditate INTO them.

Most people approach meditation backward when emotions get loud. They try to push the anger, the sadness, the anxiety out of the way so they can "get back to calm." That fight is exactly what keeps the feeling stuck. The emotion gets louder because you're refusing to be with it.

Here is the actual technique for sitting with intense emotion.

→ Step 1. Name it. Out loud or silently — "anger," "sadness," "fear," "shame." UCLA brain scans show that simply labeling an emotion reduces amygdala activity and engages the prefrontal cortex. The brain calms the moment you name what's happening. You are not the emotion. You are the one noticing it.

→ Step 2. Locate it in your body. Every emotion has an address. Anger lives in the jaw and chest. Sadness sits heavy in the stomach or throat. Anxiety buzzes in the chest and shoulders. Close your eyes and scan — where is the sensation physically living right now?

→ Step 3. Breathe into that exact spot. Slow inhale, slow exhale, and direct the breath into the area you found. You are not trying to flush the feeling out. You are giving it room to exist. Tight things soften when they are not being fought.

→ Step 4. Observe, don't fix. This is the hardest part. Do not try to make the feeling smaller, faster, or different. Just watch it. Notice how it shifts. Notice how it tightens, loosens, moves, and changes shape on its own. Emotions are waves — they crest and they fall. They cannot do that if you're holding them down.

You did not fail meditation because a feeling showed up. The feeling showing up IS the meditation.

Sit with it. Every wave passes. You're still here.
