# Day 48 Caption — "Kapalabhati: the skull-shining breath" (TUTORIAL)

## TikTok (@stillmeditation)

Skip the coffee. Try this 90-second breathing trick instead.

It is called Kapalabhati. Sanskrit for "skull-shining breath." Yogis have been doing it for three thousand years. It clears the head, lights up the body, and resets your focus without putting another stimulant into your bloodstream. Save this. You will use it the next time the 3pm crash hits.

Here is the full setup.

→ Step 1. Posture. Sit tall on the floor or in a chair. Spine straight. Crown lifting toward the ceiling. Hands resting on your knees, palms down. Take one slow normal breath through the nose to settle.

→ Step 2. The exhale. This is the whole technique. Short sharp exhales through the nose. Each exhale is driven by your belly snapping inward — like a quick punch from the inside. Your mouth stays closed the entire time. The exhale is loud and snappy. Aim for one snap per second.

→ Step 3. The inhale. Do not pull air in. The belly releases after each snap and the lungs refill on their own — this is the trick most people miss. Active exhale, passive inhale. If you are forcing the inhale, slow down.

→ The dose. Twenty pumps per round. Then a full breath in and a long slow breath out as rest. Two rounds total for beginners. That is ninety seconds of work.

→ Why it works. The forced exhales dump CO2 fast and bring fresh oxygen into your bloodstream at a rate normal breathing never reaches. Adrenaline rises briefly. Brain blood flow shifts. You feel alert, clear, slightly buzzing. Without caffeine. Without the crash four hours later.

→ Safety — read this. Do not do this practice if you are pregnant, have uncontrolled high blood pressure, heart disease, glaucoma, epilepsy, panic disorder, or had recent abdominal surgery. Never do it standing up, driving, or in water. If you feel dizzy, stop and breathe normally. This is a stimulating practice. It is not for everyone. When in doubt, talk to your doctor.

When it is safe for you, it works on the first try.

Two rounds. Ninety seconds. Watch what happens.
