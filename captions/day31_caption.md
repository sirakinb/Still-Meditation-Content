# Day 31 Caption — "This 3,000-year-old technique balances your brain hemispheres" (TUTORIAL)

## TikTok (@stillmeditation)

There is a breathing technique older than most of human history.

Yogis called it nadi shodhana. The West calls it alternate nostril breathing. Modern researchers call it one of the fastest ways to shift your nervous system on demand.

The mechanism is simple. Each nostril is wired to a different branch of your autonomic system. The left nostril biases the parasympathetic — the calm, rest-and-digest side. The right nostril biases the sympathetic — the alert, active side. Switching between them on purpose forces your body to balance the two.

Here is the technique.

→ Step 1. Sit upright. Bring your right hand to your face. Use your right thumb to close your right nostril. Inhale slowly through your left nostril for a count of four.

→ Step 2. Close your left nostril with your right ring finger. Release your thumb. Exhale slowly through your right nostril for a count of four.

→ Step 3. Inhale through the right for four. Close the right with your thumb. Release the ring finger. Exhale through the left for four.

That is one full cycle. Repeat for five to ten minutes.

What changes. Heart rate variability improves within minutes. Blood pressure drops. Stress hormones fall. Studies show measurable improvements in attention and reaction time after a single session.

It is the closest thing to a hardware reset your nervous system has.

Try one round before your next stressful task. Notice the difference.
