# Day 76 Caption — "Non-dual awareness: who is meditating?" (TUTORIAL)

## TikTok (@stillmeditation)

Turn your attention around and look for the one who is looking.

Most people meditate for years without ever doing this. They watch their thoughts, they follow the breath, they observe sensations — but they never stop to ask: who is doing all of this? The moment you turn that question on itself, everything shifts.

This is called non-dual awareness. And Sam Harris — neuroscientist, philosopher, and longtime meditator — calls it the single most important insight meditation can deliver.

→ Step 1: Settle in. Close your eyes. Let thoughts arise without trying to control them. Breathe naturally for two to three minutes until the mind begins to quiet.

→ Step 2: Ask the question. Silently ask: "Who is aware of these thoughts right now?" Do not think about the question. Just let it land. Then turn your attention back on itself — not outward toward thoughts, but inward toward the one who is noticing.

→ Step 3: Look carefully. Look for the meditator. Search for the fixed self that is supposedly doing all this watching. You will find sensations. You will find thoughts. But you will not find a solid, separate thing that is "you" doing the observing.

→ What you find instead. Just open awareness. A field of knowing with no one at the center. Not emptiness in the bleak sense — more like vast, quiet space that thoughts move through without sticking.

This is not a philosophical trick. It is a direct pointer. The noticing itself is the practice. You do not have to understand it. You just have to look.

There is no meditator. Only meditation.
