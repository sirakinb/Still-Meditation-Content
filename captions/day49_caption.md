# Day 49 Caption — "How to transition from breathwork into meditation" (QUICK TIP)

## TikTok (@stillmeditation)

The secret to deeper meditation? Start with breathwork.

Most people sit down to meditate, get hit by a wall of mental noise, and quit. Then they decide they "can't meditate." The truth is the mind was never going to settle from a cold start. You have to clear the runway first. A short round of breathwork is how you do that — and Stanford research backs this up. Save this so you actually use it tonight.

Here is the four-minute protocol.

→ Step 1. Three minutes of breathwork. Pick one. Box breathing — four in, four hold, four out, four hold. Or coherent breathing — five in, five out. Slow, even, through the nose. Do not skip this. The breathwork is the part that clears mental clutter and downshifts your nervous system into a meditative state.

→ Step 2. One minute of transition. Stop counting. Stop controlling the breath. Just watch it. Feel the air at the nostrils, the rise of the belly, the soft pause between inhale and exhale. This is the bridge. You are dropping from active to passive.

→ Step 3. Meditate. Whatever your practice is — focus on the breath, body scan, mantra, open awareness. You will notice something immediately: the mind is already quiet. You drop in fast. Sessions that used to take ten minutes to settle into now start the moment you close your eyes.

→ Why this works. A 2023 Stanford study compared breathwork and mindfulness meditation head to head. Breathwork improved mood and reduced anxiety MORE than meditation alone. Combine them and you get both. Breathwork resets the body. Meditation rests the mind. The order matters.

→ The big shift. You stop fighting your mind. You stop forcing focus. The breathwork does the heavy lifting and meditation becomes the easy part. This is what experienced practitioners do every single time.

Four minutes. Try it tonight.
