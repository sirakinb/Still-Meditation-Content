# Day 40 Caption — "Meditation is selfish" (MYTH)

## TikTok (@stillmeditation)

Taking 10 minutes to sit still isn't selfish. It's strategic.

The guilt around meditating is the most counterproductive feeling in wellness. You skip the practice that would make you a better partner, parent, coworker, and friend — because you feel bad about taking the time. So everyone gets the depleted, reactive version of you instead.

Here is what the research actually says.

→ The oxygen mask rule is real. Airlines tell you to put yours on first for a reason — you cannot help anyone if you are running on empty. A regulated nervous system is the precondition for everything else you do for the people you love.

→ Meditators are measurably more compassionate. In one Harvard and Northeastern study, people who completed an 8-week meditation program were 50% more likely to give up their seat to a stranger in pain than non-meditators. The control group barely moved. Meditation did not make people self-absorbed. It made them more available.

→ You become less reactive. Regular meditators show reduced amygdala activity, which means smaller emotional flare-ups. You snap less. You listen more. You stop taking everything personally. Everyone around you gets a calmer, more patient version of you.

→ Loving-kindness meditation literally trains generosity. Brain scans show that just a few weeks of metta practice strengthens the neural circuits involved in empathy and prosocial behavior. Kindness is a skill — and like any skill, it gets stronger with reps.

A burnt-out you helps no one. A regulated you changes the room.

Sitting in stillness for 10 minutes is not stealing from the people who need you. It is the most direct way to show up as the person they actually need.
