# Day 37 Caption — "The immune system boost you didn't know breathwork gives you" (SCIENCE)

## TikTok (@stillmeditation)

Breathwork can train your immune system. This is not woo.

In 2014, researchers at Radboud University ran a study that broke a rule biologists had treated as untouchable for a century — voluntary control of the human immune response was considered impossible.

Then 24 people walked into the lab and changed that.

→ The setup. Researchers injected endotoxin — a piece of bacterial cell wall that triggers a controlled, predictable immune flare-up — into 24 healthy volunteers. Twelve had spent ten days training in cold exposure, meditation, and cyclic breathwork. Twelve were untrained controls. Same dose. Same conditions. Two very different bodies.

→ The numbers. The trained group showed 200% higher anti-inflammatory cytokines (IL-10) and 50% lower pro-inflammatory cytokines (TNF-alpha, IL-6, IL-8) than controls. They also reported far fewer flu-like symptoms — less fever, less headache, less nausea. Their immune systems did not overreact to the threat.

→ The mechanism. The breathwork piece is the engine. Cyclic hyperventilation spikes adrenaline above the level you would see during a maximum cardio session. That adrenaline binds to receptors on immune cells and tells them to dial inflammation down. Your nervous system is talking directly to your immune system.

→ What it means. You have direct, voluntary access to a system science used to call automatic. Not just calmer mood. Measurable shifts in the actual chemistry of your blood. The autonomic nervous system is not as autonomic as the textbook said.

→ The protocol. 30 to 40 deep fast breaths through the nose. Full exhale. Hold the empty until your body asks for air. Inhale, hold for 15 seconds. Repeat for three rounds. Ten minutes total. Dose dependent — more practice, more response.

This is not a cure for disease. It is not a replacement for medical care. But the door it opens is real. Your breath has a key to a room scientists once said was locked.
