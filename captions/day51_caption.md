# Day 51 Caption — "Open awareness: meditation without an anchor" (TUTORIAL)

## TikTok (@stillmeditation)

Most meditation teaches you to focus on something. The breath. A mantra. A body part. But there is a more advanced practice that does the opposite. You let go of the anchor entirely and just become the awareness itself.

It is called open awareness. Once you can hold it, regular meditation feels small. Save this and try it tonight after your normal sit.

→ What it is. Open awareness is meditation without an object. No breath focus. No mantra. No body scan. You sit, you settle, and then you release the focus. Awareness rests on nothing in particular. It just stays open, like a wide field with no fence.

→ The sky metaphor. Picture the sky. Clouds move through it. Storms move through it. The sky does not chase the clouds and it does not block them. It just holds them. That is what your mind can do with thoughts, sounds, and sensations once you stop grabbing.

→ Step 1: settle. You cannot start in open awareness. You start with the breath. Three slow minutes. Let the body soften. Let the mind drop a gear. This is the runway.

→ Step 2: release. After three minutes, let go of the breath. Stop trying to focus on anything. Open your awareness to the whole field — sound, body, thought, room, all at once. No center. No target.

→ The one rule. Do not grab. Do not push away. A car horn arises, you notice it, it passes. A thought about tomorrow arises, you notice it, it passes. An itch in your knee arises, you notice it, it passes. You witness everything and follow nothing.

→ What you will notice. The first few minutes feel weird. Your mind wants a job. It will keep reaching for the breath out of habit. Let it. Then release again. Eventually a quieter, wider quality of attention shows up. That is the practice working.

→ Why it matters. Open awareness trains the part of you that can stay steady when life moves. You stop being the cloud. You start being the sky.

Try it tonight. Five minutes after your normal sit.
